English

There is a serious discrepancy in the cell viability data presented for quercetin (Que) treatment in Figure 4B and Figure S3D. The data points and bar graphs clearly indicated that the cell viability in the "TAC + Que" group is lower than in the "TAC" group alone. This is directly opposite to the stated conclusion in the text, which claimed that Que exhibited a protective effect. This inconsistency is observed in both the main figure and the supplementary figure, suggesting it is not an isolated error. If the data is correct as plotted, it would imply that quercetin exacerbates TAC-induced cell death rather than alleviating it, which would fundamentally contradict the core narrative of the paper regarding quercetin's efficacy. The authors must urgently clarify this discrepancy.

English

The therapeutic strategy relies on high extracellular Prdx1 for targeting and high elastase for antibody release, both hallmarks of SAP. However, since Prdx1 release and elevated elastase occur in other inflammatory conditions. Moreover, it can be observed from Fig6C and Fig6F that Ab-Fc-EL-EV@Que has high accumulation in the liver and spleen. Could the authors explain whether the system could be inadvertently activated at non-pancreatic inflammatory sites, leading to the unintended release of the anti-Prdx1 antibody in non-target tissues? It is strongly recommended to provide liver and kidney function markers(AST, ALT, BUN,Cr), H&E staining of major organs (e.g., heart, liver, spleen, lung, kidney), serum and pancreas levels of key pro-inflammatory cytokines (e.g., IL-1β, IL-6, TNF-α) in the SAP model mice from different treatment groups to further confirm safety and effectiveness.
